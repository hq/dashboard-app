# Risks & Unknowns

## Data migration
- **Simpleview data export**: We don't yet know what export formats Simpleview supports or how clean the data will be. If exports are limited, we may need to scrape content from the public site.
- **Content volume**: Large content databases (thousands of events/listings) can make migration time-consuming and error-prone.
- **Relational data**: Listings linked to categories, venues, neighborhoods — these relationships need to be mapped and preserved.

## SEO
- **URL changes**: Any URL structure changes risk losing organic search traffic. Need a comprehensive redirect plan.
- **Structured data**: If the current site has rich schema.org markup, the rebuild needs to match or exceed it.

## Scope
- **Hidden complexity**: Tourism sites often have seasonal content logic, member/partner portals, and booking integrations that aren't obvious from the public-facing pages.
- **Simpleview CRM**: If the CRM is deeply integrated, replacing it may be a larger effort than expected.

## Timeline
- **End of 2026 is firm**: If scope exceeds estimates, the timeline pressure could force compromises. The two-scenario approach (2026 aggressive / 2027 full) addresses this.
